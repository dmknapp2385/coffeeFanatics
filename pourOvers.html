Pour Overs

What Is A Pour Over?

A pour over is a manual brewing method that allows for a higher level of control over the resulting coffee. This method utilizes a cone which holds a filter and coffee grounds, in which hot water is slowly poured into in a sprial pattern over the grounds. The control over the resulting coffee's taste and strength can be adjusted by changing the water temperature, pouring speed, grind size, and more!

This brewing technique differs from that of others like French Press by the extraction process. Pour Overs use a drip extraction, separated by a paper filter, whereas in a French Press, the grounds are steeped in water for a set amount of time, then is separated using a final metal mesh. Since the grounds are in contact with the water for a longer time, French Press is typically stronger than Pour Over coffee. French Presses also tend to be more grainer than Pour Overs because of the use of a metal mesh instead of a paper filter.

If you prefer a lighter and cleaner finished coffee, a Pour Over is the way to go!

Different Types of Pour Over Drippers

Chemex

Chemex is a classic pour over dripper, having its distinguished hourglass body. The hourglass is great for heat retention, making it a good choice for larger batches of coffee.

Melitta

A Melitta dripper is a simple and easy tool for Pour Overs. It is a small cone shaped dripper with a singular hole at the bottom. This dripper is usually found in plastic or ceramic, and has a spiral ribbed design similar to the Hario V60 design.

Hario V60

A Hario V60 dripper is characterized by its spiral ribbed walls. This design allows for air flow while at the same time guiding the water, resulting in an optimal brewing process.

Kalita Wave

A Kalita Wave dripper is as shown in the picture, usually found in stainless steel. This dripper design is unique in its design of 3 small holes at the bottom its flat bottom. The flat bottom and holes help ensure even saturation of the coffee grounds, and keeps the flow of water controlled.

How To Make the Perfect Pour Over!

  1. Make sure you are ready to begin brewing your Pour Over with all of the right materials. You will need a Pour Over cone, a filter, coffee grounds, a kettle, and your favorite mug!
  2. Grind size: in order for optimal extraction and avoiding bitterness, medium grind size is ideal.
  3. Water temperature: 195 - 205 degrees F is the range you should aim for so that you don't scald your coffee grounds.
  4. Coffee to Water Ratio: Typically, a 1:16 ratio of coffee to water is used. (Ex. 20 grams of coffee would require 320 grams of water)
  5. Brewing technique: Take your time when pouring the water over your grounds! This is where the flavor is made. Blooming is the first and a crucial part to the Pour Over method. Blooming is when the hot water is poured over the coffeee grounds and carbon dioxide is released, and the coffee swells. During this stage, double the amount of water to coffee should be used (Ex. if you have 20 grams of coffee, pour 40 mL of hot water). After pouring the water, allow the bloom to settle for about 45 seconds before continuing the brewing process with the rest of your water. The pouring method for the rest of your hot water should start in the middle, slowing working your way to the outer edges of the coffee grounds in a slow spiral motion. This method promotes an even and consistent extraction of the coffee grounds.
  6. Pour from kettle into your favorite mug and enjoy your delicious Pour Over coffee!
