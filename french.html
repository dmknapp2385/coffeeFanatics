French Press

French Press Facts from the Fanatics

A French press is a tool for extracting liquid coffee from solid beans. It vaguely resembles a kettle, but it is not one.

There has been coffee in France for somewhere between 50 and 700 years. More coffee could even be added to France before the quantity there creates a problem for transportation services and hospitals.

The modern day French press was created by Italians.

How to French Press

  1. 1. Preheat the coffee press and the coffee mug that you'd like to use. Bring out your beans.
  2. 2. Measure your coffee. Use the ratio of 8 grams of coffee to 4 ounces of water.
  3. 3. Boil some water. Allow the boiled water to sit for 30 to 45 seconds.
  4. 4. Add your measured coffee grounds into the French press carafe, then pour in the hot water.
  5. 5. Stir.
  6. 6. Place the lid on the French press. Wait four minutes.
  7. 7. After four minutes, slowly depress the top button of the press. This pushes the grounds to the bottom, leaving the top filled with just the liquid.
  8. 8. Pour and cherish.
