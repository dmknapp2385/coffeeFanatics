What is a French Press, Anyway?


A French press is a tool for extracting liquid coffee from solid beans. The Oxford Learner’s Dictionary defines France as “​a country in western Europe”. Coffee Fanatics might be concerned at the lack of coffee in the previous sentence, but worry not – there has been coffee in France for somewhere between 50 and 700 years. In fact, more coffee could be added to France before the quantity there creates a logistical problem for transportation services and hospitals.


The modern day “French” press was actually created by Italians.

Materials Needed

  • Hot Water
  • Ground Coffee
  • A French Press Machine

How to French Press

  1. Grind your beans. A coarser grind is recommended for french press, as is a medium to dark roasted coffee bean
  2. Fill an empty french press with the ground beans, weighing them for the perfect brew
  3. Pour in a small amount of boiling water. Allow the water and coffee to sit for 30 seconds. This allows the coffee to "bloom", which means it realeases pent up gases within the beans.
  4. Fill up the french press adding enough water until you get a ratio of 1 part coffee grounds to 15 parts water
  5. Place the frenh press top on, but do not decompress the filter. Allow the coffee to steep for five minutes, then decompress the filter and pour out the perfect cup
