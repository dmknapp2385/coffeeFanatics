What is a French Press, Anyway?


A French press is a tool for extracting liquid coffee from solid beans. The Oxford Learner’s Dictionary defines France as “​a country in western Europe”. Coffee Fanatics might be concerned at the lack of coffee in the previous sentence, but worry not – there has been coffee in France for somewhere between 50 and 700 years. In fact, more coffee could be added to France before the quantity there creates a logistical problem for transportation services and hospitals.


The modern day “French” press was actually created by Italians.
